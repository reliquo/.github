# Reliquo

Reliquo is an evidence-based genealogical research tool. It helps researchers
work the way professional genealogists do: capture sources, extract claims
from them, correlate people and events across sources, and let conflicting
evidence be resolved — rather than overwritten — into a family tree.

## Why Reliquo

Most family tree software treats a "fact" as a single mutable field: you type
a birth date, and the previous one is gone. Reliquo instead keeps every claim
tied to the source it came from. When two sources disagree, both claims are
kept, and the tree reflects a reasoned resolution between them rather than a
guess. This means your research stays auditable — you can always trace a
fact on the tree back to the record it came from.

The workflow follows the natural shape of genealogical research:

1. **Sources** — upload and organize documents, images, and records tied to
   a research project.
2. **Extraction** — pull verbatim excerpts and structured claims out of a
   source (including OCR for scanned documents).
3. **Correlation** — link the same person or entity as they appear across
   multiple sources.
4. **Tree resolution** — reduce correlated claims, using automated rules and
   researcher overrides, into a unified family tree.

## Privacy-first

Each research project's data is kept isolated from every other project, so
your research stays yours.

## Status

Reliquo is under active development and not yet ready for general use.
